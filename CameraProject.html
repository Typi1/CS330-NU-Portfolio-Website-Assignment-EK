Underwater 3D Interactive Scene and Camera Controls

Video Demo of Interactive Scene and Camera

Overview

For a project in my introduction to computer graphics class (COMP_SCI 351-1 at Northwestern University), I created an animated, interactive scene modeled after life underneath the sea. I coded models for the crustacean, fish, and strange tree-like lifeforms and rigged them to move as shown in the video demo above. The viewing port on the left side of the window is a projective view of the scene and the viewing port on the right is an orthonormal view of the scene. Both views are controlled using the camera I implemented.

Controls

  • The camera angle is controlled using the arrow keys, which tilt the camera in the corresponding direction.
  • The camera position is controlled using the WASD keys. The W and S keys have the camera go towards or away from its current line of sight respectively. The A and D keys have the camera strafe in the corresponding direction based on its current line of sight.
  • The spacebar key can be held to enable the crustacean's tail's movement. Letting go of the key will pause the animation.
  • Holding left-click on the viewport and moving the mouse will rotate the crustacean model.
