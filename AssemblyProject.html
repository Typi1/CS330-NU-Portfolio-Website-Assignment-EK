Spaceship Shooter Game Made in Assembly

Video Demo of Game

Overview

For my final project in the class "Fundamentals of Computer System Software" (COMP_ENG 205 at Northwestern University), I used the MASM assembly programming language to create a game where the player controls a spaceship fighting a boss while dodging their attacks. In the following sections, I describe the controls, objective, and mechanics of the game.

Controls

  • Rotate the spaceship with the left and right arrow keys.
  • Move forwards or backwards depending on what way the spaceship is facing with the up and down arrow keys.
  • If you have a laser powerup (indicated by the hearts in the bottom right corner being blue), press spacebar to fire a powerful laser in front of the spaceship that continues to shoot while you move.
    • Note that after firing the laser, a powerup must be collected to use it again. Not having the laser usable is indicated by the hearts in the bottom right corner being red.
  • Press/hold E to shoot bullet projectiles in front of the spaceship.
  • Press Q to pause/unpause the game.
  • Press P while the player's laser is being fired to see the hitboxes used to calculate collision with it.

  • In the top-left corner, various icons will appear.
    • The most top-left icon indicates that you have been hit and you will be invincible for as long as it is displayed (not long).
    • The icon below that indicates that your bullet projectiles have hit the enemy.
    • The icon to the right of that indicates that your laser has hit the enemy.
    • Note that these icons disappear when the conditions that make them appear become unsatisfied.
  • When you have been hit and you health is now represented by an odd number of hearts, a powerup will appear in the top region of the screen.
    • Collect this powerup to regain a charge of the laser ability. Note that only 1 can be stored at a time.
    • Additionally, powerups will not spawn during the 4th phase, though if one spawned in a previous phase and wasn't collected, it will still be there.
  • If you game over or clear the game and want to play again, relaunch the .exe file.

Objective

  • Defeat the enemy in the center of the screen by damaging it by hitting it with the bullet projectiles and player's laser.
  • Be mindful not to run out of health, indicated by the hearts in the bottom right corner. You can be damaged by getting hit by the enemy's laser or bullet projectiles.
    • Note that you won't get hurt by touching the enemy itself.
    • Running out of health results in a game over and you must restart by relaunching the .exe file.
  • The enemy has four phases:
    1. A rigid pattern of vertical and horizontal lasers appear onscreen. A brief look at where the lasers will fire will appear before the laser hitboxes are active so you can avoid them.
    2. The enemy will fire its bullet projectiles in 4 directions at once and slowly rotate its aim for all of them.
    3. The enemy does mostly the same behavior as in phase 2 but its rotation arc is wider and it rotates quicker.
    4. Phases 1 and 2 occur simultaneously.
  • You can tell that a phase change has occurred by the change in attack patterns and the enemy's appearance changing.
